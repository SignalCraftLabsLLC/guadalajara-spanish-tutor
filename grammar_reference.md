# NIVEL A1 — Principiante

*A1 establishes the core building blocks: how sentences are structured, noun gender, pronouns, numbers, basic negation, and present tense verbs.*

---

## A1.1 Pronombres Personales (Subject Pronouns)

🌵 **Mexican Spanish uses USTEDES for all plural "you" — formal AND informal.** Vosotros does not exist here.

| Person | Pronoun | Mexican Note |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| 1st singular | yo | |
| 2nd singular informal | tú | standard tuteo |
| 2nd singular formal | usted | + 3rd-person singular verb |
| 3rd singular | él / ella | |
| 1st plural | nosotros / nosotras | |
| 2nd plural (ALL) | ustedes 🌵 | replaces vosotros entirely |
| 3rd plural | ellos / ellas | |

Subject pronouns are **often omitted** in Spanish (the verb ending signals person):
*Como tacos. / Vivimos en Guadalajara. / ¿Dónde trabajas?*

Pronouns are used for **contrast or emphasis only:**
*Yo como carne, pero ella es vegetariana.*

🌵 **Usted** is used more broadly in Mexico than in Spain. Use it with strangers, service workers, elders, and anyone you want to show respect — even if they're not much older than you. → catalog §28

---

## A1.2 Género Gramatical (Noun Gender)

Spanish nouns are masculine (m) or feminine (f). No neutral. Adjectives and articles must match.

**Common patterns (not absolute rules):**

| Pattern | Gender | Examples |
|---------|--------|---------|
| ends in -o | masculine | el libro, el médico, el carro |
| ends in -a | feminine | la casa, la mesa, la farmacia |
| ends in -ción / -sión | feminine | la nación, la canción, la tensión |
| ends in -dad / -tad / -tud | feminine | la ciudad, la libertad, la salud |
| ends in -ma (Greek origin) | masculine | el problema, el sistema, el tema |
| ends in -e | either | el nombre / la leche (must memorize) |
| ends in consonant | either | el hospital / la ciudad (must memorize) |

**Important exceptions:**
- *el agua, el águila* → feminine noun taking *el* before stressed *a-* → catalog §13
- *el día, el mapa, el idioma* → end in -a but masculine

→ catalog §13 (articles), §12 (adjective agreement)

## A1.3 Negación (Negation)

**Simple negation:** place *no* directly before the conjugated verb.
*No hablo inglés. / No tengo hambre. / ¿No comes carne?*

**Double negation (required in Spanish):**
When a negative word follows the verb, *no* still precedes the verb:
*No como nada. / No veo a nadie. / No voy nunca.*
(If the negative word comes first, *no* is dropped: *Nada como. / Nadie vino. / Nunca voy.*)

| Negative Word | Opposite |
|---------------|----------|
| nunca / jamás (never) | siempre (always) |
| nadie (nobody) | alguien (somebody) |
| nada (nothing) | algo (something) |
| ningún / ninguna (none) | algún / alguna (some) |
| tampoco (neither/nor) | también (also) |
| ni... ni... (neither...nor...) | o... o... (either...or...) |

*No me gusta ni el café ni el té.*
*Tampoco quiero agua — no tengo sed.*

---

## A1.4 La "a" Personal (Personal "a")

When the **direct object of a verb is a specific person** (or beloved pet), place *a* before it:

*Llamo a mi mamá todos los días.*
*¿Conoces a Lalo?*
*Busco a la doctora Ramírez.*

No personal *a* with indefinite persons or things:
*Busco un médico.* (looking for any doctor — indefinite)
*Como tacos.* (thing, not person)

Verbs **tener** and **haber** do not take personal *a*:
*Tengo un hermano. / Hay un doctor aquí.*

## A1.5 Números y Ordinales (Numbers and Ordinals)

**Cardinal numbers 1–100:**

1 uno/una · 2 dos · 3 tres · 4 cuatro · 5 cinco · 6 seis · 7 siete · 8 ocho · 9 nueve · 10 diez
11 once · 12 doce · 13 trece · 14 catorce · 15 quince · 16 dieciséis · 17 diecisiete · 18 dieciocho · 19 diecinueve
20 veinte · 21 veintiuno · 22 veintidós ... 30 treinta · 31 treinta y uno ...
40 cuarenta · 50 cincuenta · 60 sesenta · 70 setenta · 80 ochenta · 90 noventa · 100 cien (ciento before another number: ciento dos)

**Gender agreement:** uno/una agree with the noun.
*Tengo un hermano y una hermana. / Veintiún pacientes / veintiuna personas.*

**Ordinal numbers 1–10:**
primero/a · segundo/a · tercero/a · cuarto/a · quinto/a · sexto/a · séptimo/a · octavo/a · noveno/a · décimo/a

*Vivo en el tercer piso. / Es la primera vez que vengo.* (primero/tercero shorten before m. sing. nouns)

## A1.6 Días, Meses, Estaciones (Days, Months, Seasons)

**Days of the week** (lowercase in Spanish, all masculine):
lunes · martes · miércoles · jueves · viernes · sábado · domingo

*El lunes tengo cita. / Los lunes trabajo.* (definite article = every Monday)

**Months** (lowercase, all masculine):
enero · febrero · marzo · abril · mayo · junio · julio · agosto · septiembre · octubre · noviembre · diciembre

**Seasons:**
la primavera (spring) · el verano (summer) · el otoño (autumn) · el invierno (winter)

*En Guadalajara, la temporada de lluvias es de junio a septiembre.* 🌵

**Dates:** *Hoy es el quince de junio de dos mil veintiséis.*
*¿En qué fecha nació? — El tres de mayo.*

---

## A1.7 Colores (Colors)

los colores: rojo/a · azul · amarillo/a · verde · naranja · morado/a · violeta · rosa (invariable) · negro/a · blanco/a · gris · café/marrón 🌵 · beige · dorado/a · plateado/a

🌵 In Mexico, **café** is the standard word for brown (color), not *marrón* (which is used in Spain).

Adjectives agree in gender and number: *la camisa roja / los zapatos negros / la blusa verde*
Invariable colors (end in a vowel other than -o/-a, or are nouns used as adjectives):
*azul, verde* → only change for plural: *los pantalones azules*
*rosa, naranja, beige* → invariable: *las rosas rosa / los zapatos naranja*

---

## A1.8 Estructura de la Oración (Sentence Structure)

**Basic word order: SUBJECT + VERB + OBJECT/COMPLEMENT**
*El médico examina al paciente. / María vive en Guadalajara.*

**Yes/No questions:** invert verb and subject OR use rising intonation (very common in speech):
*¿Come usted bien? / ¿Tienes hambre?* → catalog §11 for question words

**Answers to yes/no questions:**
*Sí, como bien. / No, no tengo hambre.* (repeat the verb in the answer)

🌵 **Spoken Mexican Spanish** relies heavily on intonation for questions — written ¿...? markers are essential in writing but in speech a raised tone does the work.

→ catalog §3 (regular present tense), §6–§9 (irregular verbs)

---

## A1.9 Verbos Modales + Infinitivo (Modal Verbs + Infinitive)

These verbs are conjugated; the following verb stays in **infinitive**:

| Verb | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| querer (e→ie) | to want to | *Quiero comer tacos.* |
| poder (o→ue) | to be able to / can | *¿Puedes venir mañana?* |
| deber | ought to / should | *Debes tomar el medicamento.* |
| necesitar | to need to | *Necesito descansar.* |
| tener que | to have to (obligation) | *Tienes que hacer ejercicio.* |
| hay que | one must (impersonal) | *Hay que tomar agua.* |

*tener que* is personal (conjugated); *hay que* is impersonal (invariable):
*Tengo que llamar al doctor. / Hay que hacer una cita.*

→ catalog §29 (*ir a* + infinitive — near future)

## A2.1 Verbos de Cambio de Estado (Verbs of Change + Infinitive)

Verbs that express **beginning, continuation, or ending** of an action:

| Structure | Meaning | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| empezar a + inf. | to start doing | *Empecé a estudiar español.* |
| comenzar a + inf. | to begin doing | *Comenzó a llover.* |
| volver a + inf. | to do again | *Volví a leer el capítulo.* |
| dejar de + inf. | to stop doing | *Dejó de fumar hace un año.* |
| seguir + gerund | to keep doing | *Sigue tomando el medicamento.* |
| acabar de + inf. | to have just done | *Acabo de llegar.* |
| terminar de + inf. | to finish doing | *Terminé de comer.* |

*Acabar de* is present tense but translates as "just did":
*Acabo de hablar con el doctor.* = I just spoke with the doctor.

---

## A2.2 Conjunciones y Conectores (Connectors)

**Coordination:**
- *y* (and) · *e* (and — before words starting in i/hi: *tú e Isabel*)
- *o* (or) · *u* (or — before words starting in o/ho: *siete u ocho*)
- *pero* (but — contrast with a valid premise)
- *sino* (but rather — after a negative: *No es enfermera, sino médica.*)
- *ni... ni...* (neither...nor)

**Cause/effect:**
- *porque* (because) + conjugated verb
- *como* (since/as — fronted clause): *Como no tenía dinero, no fui.*
- *por eso* (that's why / so)

**Concession:**
- *aunque* (although/even though) + indicative (fact) or subjunctive (hypothetical)
- *sin embargo* (nevertheless)

**Time:**
- *cuando* (when) · *mientras* (while) · *antes de que* (before) · *después de que* (after)
- *desde que* (since) · *hasta que* (until)

**Condition:**
- *si* (if) → see B1.7 for si clauses

🌵 *Igual y* is a very common Tapatio connector meaning "maybe" or "perhaps":
*Igual y te llamo más tarde.* = Maybe I'll call you later. (Standard: *Quizás / Tal vez*)

---

## A2.3 Pronombre Relativo "Que" (Relative Clause Intro)

*Que* links a clause to the noun it describes (person or thing — no distinction at A2):
*El médico que me atendió es muy bueno.*
*La medicina que tomo es para la presión.*
*El hospital que está en el centro tiene urgencias.*

At A2, focus on: **NOUN + que + VERB** (subject relative) and **NOUN + que + SUBJECT + VERB** (object relative):
*La doctora que vino* (she came) vs. *La doctora que llamé* (I called her)

→ B1.6 for full relative pronoun set (quien, donde, lo que, el que...)

---

## A2.4 "Se" Impersonal

Used for **general statements where no specific person is doing the action** (equivalent to English "one/you/they/people"):

*En México se come mucho chile.* = People eat a lot of chile in Mexico.
*Aquí se habla español.* = Spanish is spoken here.
*Se trabaja mucho en esta empresa.*

**Structure:** *se* + 3rd-person singular OR plural verb (agree with grammatical subject):
*Se vende una casa.* (singular object) / *Se venden casas.* (plural object)

🌵 This is the same *se* as in reflexive verbs but with a different function — context distinguishes them.

---

## A2.5 Expresiones de Tiempo (Time Expressions)

**Hace + time + que + present** = how long something has been going on:
*Hace tres años que vivo en Guadalajara.* = I've been living in GDL for three years.
*¿Cuánto tiempo hace que estudias español?*

**Llevar + gerund** = same meaning, very common in speech:
*Llevo tres años viviendo en Guadalajara.*
*¿Cuánto tiempo llevas estudiando español?*

**Hace + time + que + preterite** = how long ago something happened:
*Hace dos horas que llegó.* = He arrived two hours ago.
*¿Cuánto tiempo hace que te operaron?*

**Desde hace + time** = since (with present tense):
*Tomo este medicamento desde hace seis meses.*

---

## A2.6 Diminutivos (-ito / -ita) 🌵

Diminutives are **extremely common in Mexican Spanish**, especially in Guadalajara. They express:
- Smallness: *un cafecito* (a small coffee)
- Affection/closeness: *mamacita, amorcito, hijita*
- Politeness/softening: *un momentito, ahorita*
- Sometimes irony/sarcasm (context-dependent)

**Formation:** attach to noun/adjective stem
- Stem ending in vowel: drop vowel, add -ito/-ita: *mesa → mesita / chico → chiquito*
- Stem ending in consonant: add -ito/-ita: *limón → limonito / señor → señorito*
- Special cases: -c → -qu (*poco → poquito*), -g → -gu (*lago → laguito*), -z → -c (*vez → vecita*)

🌵 **Ahorita** is one of the most Tapatio words you'll encounter — but it's famously ambiguous:
- *ahorita* = right now / in a moment / sometime later / never (context and tone tell you which)
- *ahorita voy* might mean "I'm on my way" or "I'll get there eventually"

🌵 **Un momentito / un ratito** = just a moment (softened with diminutive — standard politeness in GDL service culture)

---

## A2.7 Expresiones del Tiempo Atmosférico (Weather)

**Hacer + noun** for temperature and general weather:
*hace calor / hace frío / hace viento / hace sol / hace buen/mal tiempo*

**Estar + adjective** for sky conditions:
*está nublado / está despejado / está húmedo*

**Haber** (impersonal) for precipitation and visibility:
*hay niebla / hay neblina / hay tormenta*

**Weather verbs** (used in 3rd person singular only):
*llover (o→ue): llueve / llovió / llovía*
*nevar (e→ie): nieva* (rare in GDL)
*tronar (o→ue): truena* (thunder)

🌵 **Guadalajara weather note:** The city sits at 1,560m elevation. *La temporada de lluvias* (June–September) brings daily afternoon thunderstorms: *truena mucho en el verano y llueve por las tardes.* Outside that window it's almost always *despejado y soleado.*

## A2.8 Por vs. Para (Introducción)

Full mastery comes at B1, but A2 learners encounter both constantly.

**POR — think: exchange, duration, cause, agent, movement through**
- Exchange / price: *Lo compré por cien pesos.*
- Duration: *Estudié por dos horas.*
- Cause/reason: *No fui por el tráfico.*
- On behalf of: *Gracias por todo.*
- Movement through: *Pasamos por el centro.*
- Per/rate: *Dos veces por semana.*

**PARA — think: destination, deadline, purpose, recipient, opinion**
- Destination: *Salgo para el hospital.*
- Deadline: *La tarea es para el lunes.*
- Purpose: *Este medicamento es para el dolor.*
- Recipient: *Compré flores para mi esposa.*
- Opinion: *Para mí, es muy picante.* 🌵
- Direction (toward): *Camina para allá.*

🌵 *Para mí* (in my opinion) is the standard way to soften opinions in Mexican conversation.

→ B1.4 for full *por vs. para* treatment

---

## A2.9 Pretérito Perfecto — Nota Mexicana 🌵

The present perfect exists in Mexican Spanish but its use differs significantly from Spain.

**In Spain:** present perfect is used for recent or experiential actions:
*Esta mañana he tomado café. / ¿Has visto esa película?*

**In Mexico 🌵:** the **preterite (indefinido) is strongly preferred** — even for recent actions:
*Esta mañana tomé café. / ¿Viste esa película?*

This is one of the most important Mexican grammar notes for a learner: if you use *he comido* in Guadalajara, people will understand you, but *comí* is what locals say.

**When present perfect IS commonly used in Mexico:**
- Life experiences with *nunca / alguna vez*: *¿Alguna vez has ido a Tequila?* 🌵
- Medical/ongoing situations: *He estado tomando este medicamento.*

→ catalog §34 (present perfect structure) and §35 (time markers)

## B1.1 Futuro Simple (Simple Future Tense)

**Use:** predictions, promises, future plans (more formal than *ir a* + infinitive).
*Mañana lloverá en Guadalajara. / Te llamaré esta noche. / ¿Cuándo llegará el médico?*

Also used for **present conjecture** (wondering about something now):
*¿Dónde estará Lalo? — Estará en clase.* = Where could Lalo be? — He must be in class.

**Formation:** full infinitive + endings (same for -AR/-ER/-IR):
| | Ending | Example (hablar) |
|-|--------|-----------------|
| yo | -é | hablaré |
| tú | -ás | hablarás |
| él/ella | -á | hablará |
| nosotros | -emos | hablaremos |
| ellos/ellas | -án | hablarán |

**Irregular stems** (the ending stays regular — only the stem changes):

| Verb | Stem | |
|------|------|-|
| tener | tendr- | tendré |
| venir | vendr- | vendré |
| salir | saldr- | saldré |
| poder | podr- | podré |
| poner | pondr- | pondré |
| querer | querr- | querré |
| saber | sabr- | sabré |
| haber | habr- | habrá (impersonal) |
| hacer | har- | haré |
| decir | dir- | diré |
| caber | cabr- | cabré |
| valer | valdr- | valdré |

---

## B1.2 Condicional Simple (Conditional Tense)

**Use:** hypothetical situations, polite requests, would (in conditional clauses).
*¿Podrías ayudarme? / Querría una mesa para dos. / En tu lugar, iría al médico.*

Also used in indirect speech for a past "will":
*Dijo que vendría.* = He said he would come.

**Formation:** full infinitive + endings (same irregular stems as future):
| | Ending | Example (hablar) |
|-|--------|-----------------|
| yo | -ía | hablaría |
| tú | -ías | hablarías |
| él/ella | -ía | hablaría |
| nosotros | -íamos | hablaríamos |
| ellos/ellas | -ían | hablarían |

Irregular stems: same as future (tendr-, vendr-, saldr-, podr-, pondr-, querr-, sabr-, habr-, har-, dir-).

🌵 **Politeness in Mexico:** The conditional is used for formal/polite requests, but tú + conditional is also common informally:
*¿Me podrías explicar eso?* = Could you explain that to me? → catalog §28 (formality levels)

---

## B1.3 Presente de Subjuntivo (Present Subjunctive)

The subjunctive is a **mood** (not a tense) — it expresses subjectivity: wishes, doubts, emotions, recommendations. It almost always appears in a **subordinate clause introduced by "que"**.

**Formation — regular verbs:**
Take the *yo* present indicative form, drop the -o, add opposite vowel endings:

| | -AR (hablar → habl-) | -ER (comer → com-) | -IR (vivir → viv-) |
|-|----------------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| yo | hable | coma | viva |
| tú | hables | comas | vivas |
| él/ella | hable | coma | viva |
| nosotros | hablemos | comamos | vivamos |
| ellos/ellas | hablen | coman | vivan |

**Key irregulars** (yo-irregular present carries into all subjunctive forms):
*tener → tenga / venir → venga / salir → salga / hacer → haga / decir → diga / poner → ponga / conocer → conozca / construir → construya*

**Fully irregular subjunctive (memorize):**
*ser → sea/seas/sea/seamos/sean*
*estar → esté/estés/esté/estemos/estén*
*ir → vaya/vayas/vaya/vayamos/vayan*
*haber → haya*
*saber → sepa*
*dar → dé/des/dé/demos/den*

**Stem-changing verbs in subjunctive:**
- -AR/-ER stem-changers: change in all forms EXCEPT nosotros
  *pensar: piense/pienses/piense/pensemos/piensen*
- -IR stem-changers: change in all forms including nosotros (but nosotros uses the e→i or o→u change from the gerund):
  *pedir: pida/pidas/pida/pidamos/pidan*
  *dormir: duerma/duermas/duerma/durmamos/duerman*

---

## B1.4 Usos del Subjuntivo — WEIRDO + Duda

The subjunctive is triggered by a **different subject in the subordinate clause** and one of these categories:

**W — Wishes (deseos):**
*Quiero que vengas. / Espero que te mejores pronto. / Ojalá que llueva.*
🌵 *Ojalá* (from Arabic via Spanish — literally "God willing") is extremely common in Mexican speech.

**E — Emotion (emoción):**
*Me alegra que estés mejor. / Es una lástima que no puedas venir. / Tengo miedo de que sea grave.*

**I — Impersonal expressions:**
*Es importante que tomes el medicamento. / Es necesario que descansen. / Es bueno que comas bien.*

**R — Recommendation / request (recomendación):**
*El médico recomienda que haga ejercicio. / Te pido que llegues a tiempo. / Me dicen que no coma sal.*

**D — Doubt / denial (duda / negación):**
*Dudo que sea verdad. / No creo que tenga fiebre. / No es cierto que esté enfermo.*

**O — Opinion (negated):**
*No pienso que sea buena idea. / No es verdad que llegue tarde.*

**Same subject → INFINITIVE (not subjunctive):**
*Quiero COMER.* (I want to eat — same person)
*Quiero que TÚ COMAS.* (I want you to eat — different people → subjunctive)

---

## B1.5 Cláusulas Condicionales con "Si" (Si Clauses)

**Type 1 — Real/possible conditions (present tense → present or future):**
*Si comes bien, te sientes mejor.* (general truth — both present)
*Si comes bien, te sentirás mejor.* (likely result — si + present → future)
*Si tienes fiebre, llama al médico.* (si + present → imperative — instruction)

**Type 2 — Hypothetical / contrary to present fact (imperfect subjunctive → conditional):**
*Si tuviera más tiempo, aprendería más español.* = If I had more time, I would learn more Spanish.
*Si fuera rico, viviría en Guadalajara.* = If I were rich, I would live in GDL.

*(Imperfect subjunctive = past subjunctive: tuviera, fuera, pudiera, hiciera, etc. — full B1/B2 topic)*

**Note:** *Si* NEVER triggers present subjunctive (a common learner error).
*Si quieres* (not *si quieras*) → *Si quieres, vamos al mercado.*

---

## B1.6 Pronombres Relativos (Relative Pronouns — Complete)

**Que** — most common; refers to people or things (subject or object):
*El médico que me atendió. / La medicina que tomo.*

**Quien / quienes** — refers to people only; used after prepositions or in non-restrictive clauses:
*La doctora con quien hablé es especialista. / Lalo, quien es de GDL, habla muy rápido.*

**Donde** — place:
*El hospital donde me operaron está en el centro.*

**Lo que** — what / that which (neuter — refers to an idea, not a specific noun):
*No entiendo lo que dices. / Lo que me gusta de GDL es la comida.*

**El que / la que / los que / las que** — the one(s) who/which (gendered, can stand alone):
*El que llegó tarde es mi primo. / Las que quieran venir, que levanten la mano.*

**Cuyo/a/os/as** — whose (possessive; agrees with thing possessed, not the person):
*La paciente cuyo expediente busco está en urgencias.*

## B1.7 Por vs. Para — Tratamiento Completo

→ See A2.8 for introduction. Full B1 treatment:

**POR (complete uses):**

| Use | Example |
|-----|---------|
| Exchange / price | *Te lo cambio por otro.* |
| Duration | *Estudié por dos horas.* |
| Cause / reason | *Faltó por enfermedad.* |
| Agent in passive | *Fue operado por el cirujano.* |
| Movement through | *Pasamos por el mercado.* |
| Per / rate | *Tomo dos pastillas por día.* |
| On behalf of | *Firma por mí, por favor.* |
| Means / method | *Hablamos por teléfono.* |
| In favor of | *Voté por ella.* |
| Multiplication | *Tres por tres son nueve.* |

**PARA (complete uses):**

| Use | Example |
|-----|---------|
| Destination | *Este medicamento es para el dolor.* |
| Deadline | *Necesito los resultados para el viernes.* |
| Purpose / goal | *Estudio para aprender.* |
| Recipient | *Este regalo es para ti.* |
| Opinion | *Para mí, está demasiado salado.* 🌵 |
| Direction toward | *Voy para el norte.* |
| Surprise (given context) | *Para ser principiante, hablas muy bien.* |
| Employment | *Trabaja para el IMSS.* 🌵 |

🌵 **IMSS** = Instituto Mexicano del Seguro Social — the national social healthcare system. Very relevant in medical conversations in Mexico.

---

## B1.8 Voz Pasiva con "Se" (Passive Se — Full Treatment)

→ See A2.4 for impersonal *se* intro.

**Passive se** makes the agent invisible and focuses on the action or result:
*Se construyó el hospital en 1980.* = The hospital was built in 1980.
*Se venden medicamentos sin receta.* = Medications are sold without a prescription.
*Se hablan más de 60 lenguas en México.* 🌵

Verb agrees with the grammatical subject (the thing being "done to"):
*Se vende una casa.* (singular) / *Se venden tres casas.* (plural)

**True passive with SER + participio** (less common in speech, more formal/written):
*El hospital fue construido en 1980 por el gobierno.*
*Los exámenes fueron revisados por el médico.*

---

## B1.9 Discurso Indirecto (Indirect Speech)

Reporting what someone said or asked, using *que*:

**Direct → Indirect:**
*"Tengo fiebre."* → *Dijo que tenía fiebre.*
*"¿Cuándo llega el doctor?"* → *Preguntó cuándo llegaba el doctor.*
*"Toma este medicamento."* → *Me dijo que tomara este medicamento.* (command → subjunctive)

**Tense shifts when reporting past speech:**
| Direct (what was said) | Indirect (what was reported) |
|------------------------|------------------------------|
| present → | imperfect |
| preterite → | preterite (no change in Mexican Spanish) |
| future → | conditional |
| imperative → | imperfect subjunctive |

*"Descansa mucho."* → *Me dijo que descansara mucho.*
*"Volveré mañana."* → *Dijo que volvería al día siguiente.*

---

## B1.10 Superlativos (Superlatives)

**Relative superlative:** the most/least ___ of a group.
*El/la/los/las + más/menos + adjective + de*
*Es el restaurante más caro de la ciudad.*
*Guadalajara es una de las ciudades más importantes de México.* 🌵

**Irregular superlatives:**
bueno → el mejor / malo → el peor / grande → el mayor / pequeño → el menor

**Absolute superlative (-ísimo):** extremely ___ (no comparison):
*La comida está riquísima. / Hace muchísimo calor en agosto.*

🌵 **-ísimo + diminutive** stacking is very Tapatio informal speech:
*Está buenísimo.* = It's incredibly good. (Stacking: *buenísimísimo* — playful exaggeration)

# APÉNDICE — Español Mexicano vs. Español Estándar

*Key structural differences between Mexican Spanish and the "standard" taught in many textbooks.*

---

## Mex.1 Sistema Pronominal 🌵

| Feature | Spain | Mexico 🌵 |
|---------|-------|----------|
| 2nd person plural informal | vosotros + -áis/-éis/-ís | **ustedes + -an/-en** |
| 2nd person plural formal | ustedes + -an/-en | **ustedes + -an/-en** (same) |
| Result | 5 pronoun slots | **4 pronoun slots** (nosotros + ellos cover plural) |

There is no vosotros conjugation anywhere in Mexican Spanish. When textbooks show vosotros forms, skip them entirely.

---

## Mex.2 Preferencia por el Pretérito 🌵

Spain leans toward the present perfect for recent/experiential actions; Mexico leans toward the preterite for nearly everything.

| Situation | Spain | Mexico 🌵 |
|-----------|-------|----------|
| This morning | *Esta mañana he desayunado.* | *Esta mañana desayuné.* |
| Just now | *Acabo de llegar.* | *Acabo de llegar.* (same — use *acabar de*) |
| Life experience | *¿Has estado en Tequila?* | *¿Fuiste a Tequila?* OR *¿Has ido a Tequila?* (both ok) |

→ See A2.9 for full treatment.

---

## Mex.3 Tratamiento de Usted 🌵

Usted is used far more broadly in Mexico than in Spain:
- With strangers in shops, markets, transportation, medical settings
- With anyone older (even slightly older acquaintances)
- With service providers (default politeness)
- In formal writing (of course)

Using *tú* with a stranger or elder can feel abrupt. When in doubt in Guadalajara, start with *usted* and follow the other person's lead.

🌵 *¿Mande?* = pardon? / what? (the standard Mexican response when you didn't hear something — NOT *¿Cómo?* which can sound rude in some contexts) → also in dictionary

## Mex.5 Diminutivos como Marcador Cultural 🌵

Diminutives go far beyond grammar in Mexican culture — they signal warmth, informality, and relationship. A Mexican Spanish speaker will naturally use them in ways that might sound unusual to a learner of "standard" Spanish:

*¿Me da un cafecito?* (ordering coffee — the diminutive softens the request)
*Espérame un ratito.* (wait just a moment — *ratito* more polite than *rato*)
*¿Estás bien? Sí, estoy bien, ahorita llego.* 🌵

Notable: diminutives on **adverbs** (unusual in standard Spanish):
*ahorita* (*ahora* + -ita) / *cerquita* (*cerca* + -ita) / *poquito* (*poco* + -ito)

These Guadalajara-flavored forms appear throughout the dictionary in the `tapatio_notes` field.

# PARTE B — Especificación de Construcción de Oraciones (Sentence Generation Spec)

This part documents how the **Constructor de Oraciones** (`sentence_maker.html` /
`sentence_engine.js`) assembles fill-in-the-blank exercises on demand. Sentences are
**never stored** — every exercise is built at runtime from `spanish_dictionary.json`,
respecting agreement and using **only the conjugations present in the dictionary**
(present / preterite / imperfect — no future, conditional, or subjunctive). Scope is
**B2 and below**. Keep this spec and `sentence_engine.js` in sync (the engine is the
runtime authority; this document is the human-readable reference).

Difficulty has **two independent axes**: (1) **per-word** selection, via the existing
Leitner weighting (§B.7) — unchanged; (2) **structural/grammatical** complexity — which
template and which tense are offered — governed by the 9-step ladder in **§B.9**. The
axes are orthogonal: a learner on an early step still gets Leitner-weighted practice of
whatever vocabulary that step's unlocked templates can use.

## B.1 Person & Pronoun Reference Table

No vosotros (Mexican Spanish). These six "persons" drive every conjugation and pronoun.

| id | Subject | Conj. key (dictionary) | Number | Gender | Reflexive | Indirect obj. | Direct obj. |
|------|----------|------------------------|--------|--------|-----------|---------------|-------------|
| yo | yo | `yo` | sing | — | me | me | me |
| tu | tú | `tú` | sing | — | te | te | te |
| el | él | `él/ella` | sing | m | se | le | lo |
| ella | ella | `él/ella` | sing | f | se | le | la |
| nos | nosotros | `nosotros` | plur | m | nos | nos | nos |
| ellos | ellos | `ellos/ellas` | plur | m | se | les | los |

> **Subject-pronoun blanks** only use persons whose conjugated form is unambiguous —
> `yo, tú, nosotros, ellos`. The 3rd-person singular form (`él/ella/usted`) is skipped
> for subject-pronoun blanks because it maps to several subjects.

## B.2 Slot (Blank) Types

A sentence has **1–3 blanks**. Each blank has a *role*; the role determines the correct
answer, the agreement source, the multiple-choice distractors, and whether it feeds Leitner.

| Role | Tests | Answer source | Agreement with | Leitner word? |
|------|-------|---------------|----------------|---------------|
| `verb` | conjugation | dictionary present/pret/imperf | subject person + tense | yes (infinitive) |
| `infinitive` | infinitive after *ir a* | dictionary `word` | — | yes |
| `subject-pronoun` | subject pronoun | person table | verb form | no |
| `possessive` | unstressed possessive | derived (§16) | possessed noun number/gender | yes (the noun) |
| `article` | definite article | derived (§13) | noun gender/number | yes (the noun) |
| `adjective` | adjective agreement | derived from base form | noun gender/number | yes (adjective) |
| `ser-estar` | SER vs ESTAR + conjugation | dictionary `ser`/`estar` present | adjective's tag (see B.4) | yes (ser/estar) |
| `do-pronoun` | direct-object pronoun | derived (§36) | replaced noun gender/number | yes (the noun) |
| `io-pronoun` | indirect-object pronoun | person table (§37) | recipient | yes (the verb) |
| `reflexive-pronoun` | reflexive pronoun | person table (§20) | subject person | no |
| `gustar-verb` | gustar-type agreement | dictionary gustar present | postposed subject number (§18) | yes (gustar verb) |

## B.3 Agreement Rules (derivations)

- **Definite article / DO pronoun**: `el/la/los/las` and `lo/la/los/las` by noun gender
  (`f` → la/las; else lo/los) and number.
- **Indefinite article**: `un/una/unos/unas`.
- **Possessive (unstressed)**: `mi/mis`, `tu/tus`, `su/sus` agree with the *possessed*
  noun's **number** only; `nuestro/nuestra/nuestros/nuestras` agree with **number and gender**.
  Person → stem: yo→mi, tú→tu, él/ella/ellos→su, nosotros→nuestro-.
- **Adjective**: base (masc. sing.) → fem. by `-o → -a` (or add `-a` to `-dor`); plural by
  `+s` after a vowel, `+es` after a consonant. Adjectives ending in `-e` or a consonant are
  invariable by gender. (See §12, §25.)
- **Gustar-type**: the verb agrees with the **postposed grammatical subject** (the thing
  liked). Dictionary stores both forms as `"gusta / gustan"`; the engine picks singular or
  plural from that noun's number (§18, §39 for *doler*).
- **Reflexive**: dictionary stores the pronoun inline (`"me acuesto"`); the engine splits it
  into the reflexive-pronoun blank and the verb blank.

## B.4 SER vs ESTAR Selection

The copula is **not** chosen at random. It is read from the adjective's `grammar_rule`:

- `Adjective: used with SER (permanent trait)` → **ser**
- `Adjective: used with ESTAR (temporary state)` → **estar**
- `Adjective: SER (permanent) vs ESTAR (temporary) — meaning shifts` → either (random)
- any other / agreement-only tag → defaults to **ser**

This keeps sentences like *"Ellos **están** concentrados"* and *"Él **es** pelirrojo"* correct.

## B.5 Tense Cues

Front-of-sentence markers signal the tense so the learner knows which form to produce:

| Tense | Cue phrases |
|-------|-------------|
| Present | *Todos los días, Normalmente, Cada mañana, Siempre, Ahora* |
| Pretérito | *Ayer, Anoche, La semana pasada, El año pasado, Esta mañana* |
| Imperfecto | *Cuando era niño, Antes, De vez en cuando antes, En aquellos años* |
| Presente continuo | *En este momento, Ahora mismo, Justo ahora, En este preciso momento* |
| Pretérito perfecto | *Hoy ya, Esta semana, Por fin, Esta mañana ya* |

A verb is eligible for a tense only if its dictionary entry has **all five person keys** for
that tense; otherwise it is skipped (no form is ever invented). Presente continuo and
pretérito perfecto cues are **affirmative-only by design** — negation was considered and
rejected because it conflicts with their word order.

Which tenses a given template may draw from is not fixed — it is threaded in per battery
via a `ctx.tenses` map built from the current step (§B.9), so `conjVerb`, `subjPron`, and
`reflexive` only offer the tenses the learner has unlocked so far (plus a taper-weighted
preview of the next step's tense, near the gate).

## B.6 Template Catalog

Each template yields one exercise (1–3 blanks). Tested grammar in **bold**.

1. **conjVerb** — *"{cue}, {subject} ___."* → **verb conjugation** (present/pret/imperf).
2. **subjPron** — *"___ {verb} todos los días."* → **subject pronoun** (unique persons).
3. **possessive** — *"{Subject} {decir} que ___ {noun} es importante."* → **possessive**.
4. **article** — *"___ {noun} {verb}."* → **definite article** (gender/number).
5. **adjAgree** — *"{Art} {noun} {ser/estar} muy ___."* → **adjective agreement**.
6. **serEstar** — *"{Subject} ___ {adjective}."* → **SER vs ESTAR + conjugation**.
7. **ioPron** — *"{Subject} ___ {verb} {un/una noun} a María / a mis amigos."* → **indirect-object pronoun**.
8. **gustar** — *"A {experiencer} ___ ___ {el/los noun}."* → **IO pronoun + gustar agreement** (2 blanks).
9. **reflexive** — *"{cue}, {subject} ___ ___."* → **reflexive pronoun + verb** (2 blanks; routine verbs only).
10. **futuroProximo** — *"Mañana {subject} ___ a ___."* → **ir (conjugated) + infinitive** (2 blanks).
11. **doPron** — *"{Subject} {ver} {art noun}. {Subject} ___ {verb} siempre."* → **direct-object pronoun**.
12. **presenteContinuo** — *"{cue}, {subject} ___ ___."* → **ESTAR (present) + gerundio** (2 blanks).
    Non-reflexive verbs only — the dictionary's reflexive gerunds are fixed 3rd-person
    citation forms that don't generalize to other subjects, so reflexive continuous was
    deliberately left out rather than built incorrectly.
13. **preteritoPerfecto** — *"{cue}, {subject} ___ ___."* → **HABER (present) + participio** (2 blanks).
14. **dobleObjeto** — *"{Subject} {verb} {un/una noun} a {recipient}. {Subject} ___ ___ {verb}."*
    → **double-object pronoun, le/les→se + lo/la/los/las** (2 blanks). Restricted to a curated
    list of transfer verbs (`IO_VERBS`) and to `yo/tú/nosotros/ellos` to avoid 3rd-person
    ambiguity in the repeated clause.

Noun choice is biased toward concrete, readable categories (Animales, Alimentación, Familia,
Casa, Ciudad, Descripción personal) but any noun may appear. Because nouns and adjectives are
paired by grammar (not meaning), some sentences are semantically quirky (*"La cena está
deprimida"*) — this is expected and harmless: the **grammar** being tested is always correct.

## B.7 Leitner Weighting & Writeback

- **Selection weight** per word: `(6 − box)`, doubled if the card is **due**
  (`sessions_until_due ≤ 0`). So box-1/due words are chosen far more often than box-5 words
  (measured ≈ 3× over-representation), focusing practice on the least-familiar vocabulary.
- **Shared store**: reads/writes the same `localStorage` keys as the flashcards —
  `tapatio_dict_v` (dictionary) and `tapatio_leitner` (`{word: {box, due}}`).
- **One battery = one Leitner session**: `sessions_until_due` is decremented once at battery
  start (mirrors the flashcards).
- **Grading**: for each blank that maps to a dictionary word, a correct answer promotes the
  word (`box+1`, capped at 5, `due = BOX_INTERVALS[box]` where `BOX_INTERVALS=[0,0,1,3,7,15]`);
  a miss demotes it to box 1, due now. Pronoun-only blanks (subject/reflexive) have no word
  and never touch Leitner. A word appearing in several blanks is "known" only if **all** its
  blanks were correct.

## B.8 Battery Rules

- Rounds of **10**, up to **5 rounds** = **50 sentences** maximum.
- **No repeats**: deduplicated by the fully-filled sentence string across the whole battery.
- **Difficulty modes**: `typed` (free text graded by `SpellingHandler` — accent-tolerant) or
  `choice` (3–4 options per blank; the correct answer is always present; distractors are
  same-type forms — other persons of the verb, sibling pronouns/articles, wrong agreement).
- **English help** is shown **only on request** (per project tutoring rules): the Ayuda panel
  reveals each blank's answer, its dictionary gloss, and a grammar note.

## B.9 Step Ladder, Tapering & Gating

Structural difficulty advances through **9 discrete steps**, not a smooth ramp — chosen so
new grammar builds confidence rather than discouraging the learner. The order is grounded in
the actual chronological sequence Aura and Lalo taught the class (`notes_transcription.md`),
cross-checked against published L2 Spanish acquisition sequencing, **not** `grammar_catalog.md`'s
topical (non-chronological) section order.

### B.9.1 The 9 steps

| # | id | Label | Unlocks |
|---|----|-------|---------|
| 0 | `cimientos` | Cimientos | possessive, article, adjAgree, serEstar, conjVerb, subjPron — present tense |
| 1 | `gustarOI` | Gustar y objeto indirecto | ioPron, gustar |
| 2 | `reflexivos` | Reflexivos y rutina | reflexive — present tense |
| 3 | `presenteContinuo` | Presente continuo | presenteContinuo (ESTAR + gerundio) |
| 4 | `futuroProximo` | Futuro próximo | futuroProximo (ir + a + infinitivo) |
| 5 | `preterito` | Pretérito | conjVerb, subjPron, reflexive — adds preterite tense |
| 6 | `imperfecto` | Imperfecto | conjVerb — adds imperfect tense |
| 7 | `preteritoPerfecto` | Pretérito perfecto | preteritoPerfecto (HABER + participio) |
| 8 | `objetoDirecto` | Objeto directo | doPron, dobleObjeto (se lo / se la) |

Each step's `templates` list and `tenseAdds` map are **cumulative**: a learner on step *n*
has access to every template/tense introduced at steps `0..n`. Advancement is **one-way** —
no demotion back to an earlier step, even after missed answers.

### B.9.2 Tense-gating vs. template-gating

Most steps unlock a brand-new template. Three templates (`conjVerb`, `subjPron`, `reflexive`)
are instead **tense-gated**: they appear in `STEPS[0].templates` from the start (so the battery
generator always treats them as "available"), but which **tense** each may draw from is
controlled separately by `tenseAdds`, threaded into the templates at battery-build time as a
`ctx.tenses` map (e.g. `ctx.tenses.conjVerb = ['present','present','preterite',...]`, built
fresh per battery from the cumulative config). This is why `introducingStepIndex(templateId,
tense)` must check `tenseAdds` matches **before** falling back to plain `templates`-list
membership — otherwise a tense added at a later step (e.g. preterite at step 5) would be
wrongly attributed to step 0, where the template id first appears, and progress on that step
could never be counted.

### B.9.3 Tapering near the gate

Rather than a hard wall between steps, the next step's content is blended in gradually as the
learner approaches the gate, per Stan's preference for "rounded" steps:

- `taperFraction(progress) = clamp(0, TAPER_CAP, (attempts / GATE_MIN_SAMPLE) * TAPER_CAP)`,
  with `TAPER_CAP = 0.35`.
- In the weighted template pool, each unlocked template/tense gets `WEIGHT_SCALE = 3` copies;
  the **next** step's wholly-new templates get `max(1, round(WEIGHT_SCALE * taper))` copies —
  so a small, growing fraction of each battery previews what's coming, without ever exceeding
  ~35% of the weight.
- Taper-preview exercises still count as "next step" content for gating purposes (they don't
  count toward the *current* step's gate, since `introStep` correctly points at the next step).

### B.9.4 Gate logic & advancement

`recordStepProgress(progress, exercise, perBlankCorrect, stepIndex)`:

- Counts an attempt **only if** `exercise.introStep === stepIndex` — i.e. only the current
  step's own newly-introduced content counts, never review content from earlier steps and
  never taper-preview content from the next step.
- Gate opens at **≥70% accuracy** (`GATE_THRESHOLD = 0.70`) over a **minimum 15 qualifying
  attempts** (`GATE_MIN_SAMPLE = 15`).
- On advancing, `progress` resets (`attempts = correct = 0`) for the new step.
- Advancement is **one-way**: a run of wrong answers after advancing never demotes the learner.

### B.9.5 Storage & display

- State lives in its own `localStorage` key, `tapatio_sentence_step` — separate from the
  shared `tapatio_dict_v` / `tapatio_leitner` keys, so `flashcards_vocabulario.html` is
  unaffected.
- The setup screen shows the current step's label, sub-label, and a progress bar toward the
  gate, in **friendly, jargon-free Spanish** (grammar terms like "pretérito" or "reflexivos"
  are shown — Stan was taught those terms directly in class — but the Leitner-style
  box/level jargon used elsewhere in the project is not).
